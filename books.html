  • The Obstacle is the Way, Ryan Holiday
    Date Read: 2021/09/23
    Rating: Not rated
    Thoughts: "First, see clearly. Next, act correctly. Finally, endure and accept the world as it is."

    This book offers a corrective lens for one's own outlook on life. Instead of being a superficial, anxious, depressed, self-obsessed type of person, you could alternatively change your relationship with the world, and ultimately, yourself.

    Perception (John Rockefeller): Don't get emotional and worked up. Just see things for what they are and don't complain. Be present and think deeply.

    Action (Demosthenes): Get started and establish a great process. Do whatever you are doing to the best of your ability and do NOT give up. Adapt. Things aren't going to work the first time. Or the second.

    Will (Abraham Lincoln): Be disciplined and persevere. Smile no matter what happens, because life is too short and there is something to smile about and look forward to, always. Love your community and the body outside of yourself, because in the grand scheme, you aren't going to be around all that long. Make the best of it, while you can, with what you have.

    This book is repetitive and can be a bit rah-rah, but the message is there and many desperately need to hear it. If nothing else, I gained a little bit more perspective and courage from this short book.

  • Missing Microbes, Martin J. Blaser
    Date Read: 2021/09/01
    Rating: Not rated
    Thoughts: Blaser’s three pillars throughout the book:

    1. The various bacteria and microorganisms that exist in and around the human species play a vital role in human health and well-being.

    2. The blanket approach to prescribing antibiotics and increasing rates of procedures like the C-section are contributing to a growing public health crisis concerning antibiotic-resistant infections and diminished immunity and health amongst younger people.

    3. The role of our microbiomes in our health is a growing field with many areas of research. These attempts include pinpointing what microorganisms are helpful (and harmful) to our health, how to restore microbiota in people who were previously deficient, and improving treatments and outcomes without traditional antibiotics.

    Good read, but skipped a few chapters in the middle where he discusses his specific research on particular topics.
  • Show Your Work!, Austin Kleon
    Date Read: 2021/08/29
    Rating: Not rated
    Thoughts: Great practical and insightful tips about connecting with others and celebrating the work we do every day.

    1. You don’t have to be a genius - “Geniuses” are really just people who have poured themselves into their craft and been noticed for it.

    2. Process, not product - speaks for itself. It’s the process that lasts.

    3. Share something small every day

    4. Open up your “cabinet of curiosities” - be your authentic self and show that to people. Don’t be someone you’re not.

    5. Tell good stories - Way too much to write about this one. See Donald Miller’s book.

    6. Teach what you know - a point of connection and a way to learn for yourself

    7. Don’t turn into human spam - share but be self-aware.

    8. Learn to take a punch - the more you are criticized, the more it doesn’t hurt, because it’s not personal.

    9. Sell Out - don’t be afraid to monetize, duh.

    10. Stick around - it’s not over until you quit. Seriously.
